Teaching AI to Feel

A Vision Transformer (ViT-B/16) foundation model for 3D human pose estimation purely from tactile pressure maps.

The Challenge: Beyond Vision

Most AI models rely heavily on computer vision. However, in scenarios like intensive care monitoring or robotic manipulation, visual data is often occluded (e.g., a patient covered by a blanket). TouchGPT bridges this gap by translating raw tactile pressure data into complete 3D spatial awareness.

1. Sensory Input

We utilize a high-density tactile matrix (64x27 resolution) simulating a smart hospital bed or robotic skin. To demonstrate inference latency on the live website, the UI currently injects continuous noise simulations directly into the neural network.

2. Transformer Backbone

The core of TouchGPT is a modified ViT-B/16 architecture. It processes the pressure maps as non-overlapping patches, learning complex spatial correlations between pressure points without relying on any RGB cameras.

3. 3D Anatomical Output

The model decodes the transformer embeddings into 24 distinct 3D anatomical keypoints (72 dimensions), successfully reconstructing the precise posture and spatial orientation of the subject.
