Abstract

While computer vision has achieved immense breakthroughs via large-scale Foundation Models, tactile and physical-contact perception has historically remained restricted to task-specific architectures. We present TouchFormer, a self-supervised tactile foundation model built upon Google's Vision Transformer (ViT-B/16) architecture. By training on high-density pressure maps via a Masked Autoencoder (MAE) pretraining paradigm, TouchFormer learns generalized spatial physics and anatomical priors of the human body resting on soft surfaces without requiring annotated keypoint data. The pretrained backbone demonstrates rapid downstream convergence when fine-tuned, yielding state-of-the-art 3D human pose reconstruction and keypoint detection purely from tactile input.

Model Architecture & Methodology

TouchFormer models contact physics by processing raw 2D tactile arrays as visual patches. To capture the complex, non-local pressure distributions of a body resting on a flexible bed, we deploy Google's Vision Transformer (ViT-B/16) backbone inside a self-supervised Masked Autoencoder (MAE) loop.

Figure 1: The TouchFormer Schematic. Raw 64×27 physical pressure feeds undergo a 75% random patch masking step. The visible patches are serialized and processed by the Google ViT-B/16 encoder. In the downstream step, a lightweight linear regressor is fine-tuned to map output embeddings directly to 3D joint coordinates (24 anatomical nodes).

Masked Pretraining

By masking 75% of the pressure map inputs during pretraining, the model is optimized to reconstruct physical weight distributions, teaching it body shapes and weight distribution physics in a self-supervised manner.

Google ViT backbone

Leverages Google's 12-layer Vision Transformer (ViT-B/16). Initializing from ImageNet weights provides robust visual feature abstractions that accelerate downstream task learning on tactile grids.

Evaluation & Results

TouchFormer was evaluated on the 10GB PressurePose corpus. We analyzed the effects of pretraining initialization strategies to understand whether continued masking pretraining on pressure maps outperforms standard ImageNet representations.

Key Takeaways

1. Transfer Learning Benefits: Initializing the tactile encoder with weights from Google's ImageNet-trained ViT-B/16 yields a dramatic performance improvement over training a pressure-specific transformer from scratch.

2. Physical Domain Adaptation: Continuing MAE pretraining on the 64×27 pressure grid (Ours) adapts the visual features to physical pressure distribution vectors, resulting in our best SOTA error rate of 6.29 cm MPJPE.

Downstream Fields

A tactile foundation model enables broad applications across clinical monitoring and robotics:

  • Clinical Smart Beds: Non-intrusive 3D human pose tracking in ICU wards. Allows continuous monitoring for pressure ulcer (bedsore) risks and patient mobility without invading patient privacy using optical cameras.
  • Embodied Robotic skins: Equipping robotic arms with tactile matrix skins to sense human interaction and orientation, allowing for safer collaborative workspaces and physical rehabilitation support.
