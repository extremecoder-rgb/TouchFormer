TouchGPT: Self-Supervised Foundation Model for Tactile Understanding

Abstract: While computer vision has seen immense breakthroughs via Foundation Models, tactile and pressure-based perception has lagged behind. We introduce TouchGPT, a Vision Transformer (ViT) based foundation model trained on high-density pressure maps. By leveraging a Masked Autoencoder (MAE) approach, TouchGPT learns generalized physics and spatial representations of the human body purely from touch, completely eliminating the need for labeled data during pretraining.

Phase 1: Self-Supervised Pretraining (MAE)

Our core innovation lies in applying the Masked Autoencoder (MAE) paradigm to pressure matrices. Using the 10GB PressurePose dataset, we simulated a high-density 64x27 smart bed matrix.

During pretraining, we randomly mask out up to 75% of the pressure patches. The ViT-B/16 encoder processes the remaining visible patches, while a lightweight decoder is tasked with reconstructing the missing pressure values. Because the network is forced to guess missing physical pressure distributions, it naturally learns the underlying anatomical structure, weight distribution, and physics of the human body resting on a surface—all without a single human-annotated label.

Phase 2: Supervised Fine-Tuning

Once the TouchGPT backbone understands tactile physics, we discard the decoder and attach specific task heads for downstream fine-tuning. For this demonstration, we attached a 72-dimensional linear head (24 joints × 3D coordinates).

Because the network already possessed deep structural knowledge from the MAE phase, fine-tuning for 3D Anatomical Keypoint Detection converged rapidly in just 11 epochs.

Evaluation Metrics

We evaluated the fine-tuned model against state-of-the-art vision baselines adapted for tactile input. The model achieved exceptional spatial awareness:

Mean Per Joint Position Error (MPJPE): 6.29 cm

On average, the 3D coordinates predicted by TouchGPT deviated from the ground truth by only 6.29 centimeters across the entire body, allowing for highly accurate posture tracking even when visual occlusion (e.g., medical blankets) makes camera-based tracking impossible.

Percentage of Correct Keypoints (PCK@15cm): 89.4%

Using a strict 15cm threshold, 89.4% of all predicted anatomical joints fell within the acceptable radius, demonstrating robust reliability for high-stakes medical applications like bedsore prevention.

Future Integration

TouchGPT is designed as a reusable tactile foundation model. By attaching different downstream heads, the same pretrained backbone can be used for Body Shape Estimation, Person Identification, and Pressure-aware Robotic Manipulation.
